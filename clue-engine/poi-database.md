## GOLD ROOM — 1558 W Sunset Blvd, Echo Park

### 1. Echo Park Time Travel Mart / 826LA
- **Address:** 1714 W Sunset Blvd (~0.1 mi east)
- **Year:** 2008
- Front for 826LA, literary nonprofit co-founded by Dave Eggers. LA's version of SF's Pirate Supply Store. Secret door in back leads to free tutoring center for kids 6-18.
- Product line (robot milk, dinosaur eggs) conceived by Mac Barnett, Jon Korn, Stefan Bucher.
- **Clue angle:** "What does the sign at 1714 say you can buy?" / "What's behind the secret door?"

### 2. Jensen's Recreation Center
- **Address:** 1706 W Sunset Blvd (~0.1 mi east)
- **Year:** 1924
- Built by brick manufacturer Henry Christian Jensen. Architect E.E.B. Meinardus. Three stories: bowling/pool at street level, 46 apartments above.
- Rooftop sign: 28 ft wide × 17 ft tall, 1,300 red/green/white incandescent bulbs. Unlit for 50 years, restored 1997. LA Historic Cultural Monument #652 (1998).
- **Clue angle:** "How many light bulbs in the Jensen's sign?" / "What sport could you play on the ground floor in 1924?"

### 3. Keystone Studios (Mack Sennett)
- **Address:** 1712 Glendale Blvd (~0.2 mi north)
- **Year:** 1912
- First totally enclosed film studio in movie history. Where Hollywood began — before Hollywood.
- Charlie Chaplin's film debut here, 1914. Keystone Cops created 1912. Bathing Beauties 1915. Alumni: Arbuckle, Normand, Harold Lloyd, Gloria Swanson.
- Glendale Blvd was formerly Allesandro Street (named after Helen Hunt Jackson's novel "Ramona"). Renamed 1920s.
- Don Cornelius (Soul Train) purchased 1985. LA historic landmark 1982.
- **Clue angle:** "What famous comedian made his first film at 1712 Glendale Blvd in 1914?" / "What was this street called before Glendale Boulevard?"

### 4. Echo Park Lake & Lady of the Lake
- **Address:** 751 N Echo Park Ave (~0.2 mi south)
- **Year:** Lake 1868 (reservoir), park 1892. Statue 1935.
- Originally a water supply reservoir. Parks superintendent Joseph Henry Tomlinson named it "Echo Park" in 1892 for the echoes around the reservoir.
- Statue "Nuestra Reina de Los Angeles" by Ada Mae Sharpless (born Hilo, Hawaii 1904, studied in Paris). 14-ft cast stone, WPA commission. Budget-cut from bronze to stone.
- Vandalized, stored 1986. Restored 1999 with anti-graffiti sealant.
- Lotus flowers (Nelumbo nucifera) since 1920s — possibly planted by Aimee Semple McPherson's missionaries with seeds from China.
- Boathouse built 1932 with Depression-era unemployment bonds.
- **Clue angle:** "What is the statue's Spanish name?" / "What was this lake before it was a park?"

### 5. "Eyes" Mural by Ruben Soto
- **Address:** 1174 Glendale Blvd underpass (~0.15 mi north)
- **Year:** First completed 1991 (worked on since 1985)
- On public stairway across from Echo Park Lake. Series of large portraits.
- One panel memorializes Sage Stallone (Sylvester's son, died 2012).
- Repeatedly tagged/faded — Soto has restored it for 30+ years.
- **Clue angle:** "Which famous actor's son is memorialized in the mural under the Sunset overpass?"

### 6. Angelus Temple
- **Address:** 1100 Glendale Blvd (~0.25 mi north)
- **Year:** Opened January 1, 1923
- Founded by Aimee Semple McPherson. 5,300 seats. 125-ft dome — largest in North America when built. First woman to use radio for preaching.
- May 18, 1926: McPherson vanished from Venice Beach swim. City mourned; two people died searching. Reappeared 5 weeks later at Mexican border claiming kidnapping. Witnesses placed her in Carmel cottage with radio engineer Kenneth Ormiston. 7-month grand jury; charges dropped.
- Pioneered "illustrated sermon" — theatrical performances. Founded International Church of the Foursquare Gospel (8M+ members today).
- **Clue angle:** "How many seats in the domed church at 1100 Glendale?" / "Its founder disappeared in 1926 — where did she claim she'd been?"

### 7. Baxter Street Stairs
- **Address:** Baxter St & Avon St (~0.3 mi east)
- **Year:** Early 1900s
- 231 concrete steps. Baxter Street: 32% grade — steeper than any street in San Francisco.
- Built for Pacific Electric Red Car transit access. 24+ public stairways cross Echo Park's hillsides.
- Views from top: Echo Park, Silver Lake, Los Feliz, Hollywood Sign, Griffith Observatory, downtown skyline.
- **Clue angle:** "How many steps? What transit system were they built to reach?"

### 8. Elysian Park
- **Address:** 929 Academy Rd (~0.3 mi north)
- **Year:** 1886
- LA's oldest park (1886), second largest at 600 acres. "Elysian" = Greek for paradise. Originally "Rock Quarry Hills."
- Last remaining piece of original 1781 Spanish royal land grant establishing Pueblo de Los Angeles.
- Portola expedition camped at SE corner August 2, 1769 — first Europeans to see inland California.
- Hosted shooting events for 1932 Olympics. Avenue of the Palms created 1895.
- **Clue angle:** "What was Elysian Park called before it was a park?" / "What year did the Portola expedition camp here?"

### 9. Edendale / Silent Film District
- **Address:** Along Glendale Blvd, Sunset to Effie St (~0.1-0.3 mi north)
- **Year:** 1906-1920s
- Before Hollywood, Edendale was West Coast filmmaking center. Multiple studios on Allesandro Ave: Selig Polyscope, Pathe West Coast, Keystone.
- Famous Echo Park residents (various eras): Jackson Pollock, Steve McQueen, Leonardo DiCaprio, Frank Zappa, Elliott Smith, Woody Guthrie.
- **Clue angle:** "What was this neighborhood called before Echo Park?" / "Movies were invented here, not in Hollywood."

## LA CUEVITA — 5922 N Figueroa St, Highland Park

### 1. Highland Park Bowl
- **Address:** 5621 N Figueroa St (~0.2 mi south)
- **Year:** 1927
- Prohibition-era workaround: 8 bowling lanes, doctors' offices upstairs (prescriptions for "medicinal whiskey"), pharmacy downstairs to fill them, music store. One of 3 legal drinking spots on Figueroa during Prohibition.
- 1933: music store got live music permit. 1966: Joseph "Mr. T" Teresa renamed it "Mr. T's Bowl" — beloved music venue for decades.
- Restored by 1933 Group (same company that operates La Cuevita). LA's oldest operating bowling alley.
- **Clue angle:** "What year did this open, and what could you legally get a doctor's prescription for upstairs?" / "What was this place called from 1966 to 2016?"

### 2. Chicken Boy
- **Address:** 5558 N Figueroa St (~0.2 mi south)
- **Year:** Late 1960s (original); moved 2007
- 22-ft fiberglass statue: boy's body, chicken head, holding bucket. Originally for Chicken Boy restaurant at 450 S Broadway, DTLA.
- Restaurant closed 1984. Artist Amy Inouye rescued it, stored 23 years. Placed on roof of her design firm, Future Studio, in 2007.
- Governor Schwarzenegger's Historic Preservation Award 2010. "The Statue of Liberty of Los Angeles."
- **Clue angle:** "How tall is the statue on top of 5558 Figueroa?" / "Where did it stand before Highland Park?"

### 3. Highland Theatre
- **Address:** 5604 N Figueroa St (~0.2 mi south)
- **Year:** 1924 (opened March 2, 1925)
- Architect Lewis Arthur Smith, Moorish/Spanish Colonial Revival. 1,432 seats. Smith designed 28+ CA theaters (Vista, El Portal, Rialto). Died 1926.
- Grand opening film: "Lady of the Night" starring Norma Shearer (attended in person). Rooftop sign: 502 incandescent bulbs.
- Closed 2024, six days short of 100th anniversary. Purchased by Kristen Stewart in 2026.
- **Clue angle:** "What actress bought this theater in 2026?" / "What film screened at the 1925 grand opening?"

### 4. Highland Park Masonic Temple / Lodge Room
- **Address:** 104 N Avenue 56 (~0.2 mi south)
- **Year:** 1922-1923
- 25,000 sq ft Renaissance Revival by Elmore Robinson Jeffrey. Highland Park Lodge No. 382, Free and Accepted Masons, operated 60 years (1923-1983).
- Masonic frieze with symbols runs along exterior roofline. Figueroa facade: columns and balcony.
- Masons left 1983 (couldn't afford seismic retrofit). LA HCM #282 (1984). National Register 1990.
- Original Lodge Room preserved: cherry wood paneling, anaglypta wall coverings. Now a 500-capacity music venue.
- **Clue angle:** "What symbols run along the roofline at Avenue 56 and Figueroa?" / "What secret society met on the second floor for 60 years?"

### 5. Highland Park Ebell Club
- **Address:** 131 S Avenue 57 (~0.15 mi south)
- **Year:** 1913 (club est. 1903)
- Named after Adrian Ebell, German professor who traveled CA forming women's study groups. Westcoast Prairie style by Sumner P. Hunt and Silas Burns.
- Organizing space for women's suffrage movement. One of largest/most elite women's clubs in the nation.
- LA HCM #284 (1984).
- **Clue angle:** "What movement did the women of this club organize?" / "Who was Adrian Ebell?"

### 6. "Mexico-Tenochtitlan: The Wall That Talks"
- **Address:** 6037 N Figueroa St at Ave 61 (~0.1 mi north)
- **Year:** 1996
- 12-ft mural dominated by Quetzalcoatl (feathered serpent). By community artists. Features Cesar Chavez, Virgen de Guadalupe.
- Nominated for official LA landmark. Center of preservation battles against "whitewashing" by new property owners.
- **Clue angle:** "What Aztec god dominates the mural at Figueroa and Avenue 61?"

### 7. Southwest Museum of the American Indian
- **Address:** 234 Museum Dr, Mount Washington (~0.3 mi west)
- **Year:** Opened 1914
- Founded by Charles Fletcher Lummis. LA's first museum.
- Lummis walked 3,507 miles from Cincinnati to LA in 143 days (Sept 12, 1884 → Feb 1, 1885). Filed weekly dispatches to LA Times. Book: "A Tramp Across the Continent."
- Mission Revival building with tunnel entrance — visitors walk through tunnel to elevator rising to main level. Dioramas removed, now in storage.
- Lummis secured 38 acres 1907. Construction began 1912. Collection now managed by Autry Museum.
- **Clue angle:** "LA's first museum founder walked here from Ohio. How many miles? How many days?"

### 8. El Alisal (Lummis Home)
- **Address:** 200 East Avenue 43 (~0.5 mi south — borderline)
- **Year:** 1897-1910
- Lummis hand-built 4,000 sq ft house from Arroyo Seco river stone over 13 years. Named for sycamore grove on lot.
- Guests: Will Rogers, Douglas Fairbanks, Theodore Roosevelt. Lummis buried on grounds.
- Free public tours Sat-Sun 10am-3pm. LA Dept of Recreation & Parks.
- **Clue angle:** "What material did Lummis use? How many years to build?"

### 9. The Arroyo Seco
- **Address:** Western edge of Highland Park (~0.2 mi west)
- **Year:** Named 1770 by Gaspar de Portola
- "Arroyo Seco" = "dry stream" in Spanish. Misleading: groundwater flows from San Gabriel Mountains even when surface is dry.
- Tongva (Hahamogna) settlements for centuries pre-contact.
- Arroyo Seco Parkway (completed 1940) = first freeway in the United States.
- Lummis built his home from arroyo river stones. Drew Arts & Crafts movement.
- **Clue angle:** "What does 'Arroyo Seco' mean?" / "What famous American infrastructure first was built along this waterway in 1940?"

### 10. Los Angeles Police Museum
- **Address:** 6045 York Blvd (~0.3 mi east)
- **Year:** 1925 (station); museum 2001
- Restored 1925 Highland Park Police Station. Closed 1983. LA HCM #274 (Jan 1984). National Register (March 1984).
- Covers LAPD history from 1869. Founded 1989 as LA Police Historical Society.
- **Clue angle:** "What was this building on York Blvd used for from 1925 to 1983?"

## ESCALA — 3451 W 6th St, Mid-Wilshire/Koreatown

### 1. Chapman Plaza (Escala's building)
- **Address:** 3451 W 6th St (0 mi — you're in it)
- **Year:** 1928 (opened 1929)
- One of America's first drive-in grocery stores. Morgan, Walls & Clements (same firm as Wiltern). Spanish Colonial Revival with Churrigueresque detailing, arched entrances, ornate tile, iconic tower.
- Served wealthy neighborhoods: Fremont Place, Windsor Square, Hancock Park. Adjacent Chapman Park Hotel (demolished 1967) housed U.S. women's Olympic team during 1932 Games.
- LA HCM #361 (1988). Restored by Wayne Ratkovich + architect Brenda Levin (same pair who saved the Wiltern).
- **Clue angle:** "What was this building originally?" / "What Olympic team stayed next door in 1932?"

### 2. The Wiltern Theatre / Pellissier Building
- **Address:** 3790 Wilshire Blvd (~0.25 mi NW)
- **Year:** 1931
- 12-story, 155-ft tower. Blue-green glazed terra-cotta tiles. Zigzag Moderne by Stiles O. Clements. "Wiltern" = **Wil**shire + Wes**tern**.
- Named for Germain Pellissier, French immigrant landowner. Opened as Warner Brothers Western Theater. Interior by G. Albert Lansburgh, murals by Anthony Heinsbergen. ~2,000 seats.
- Nearly demolished twice (late 1970s). Saved by preservationists. Restored 1981-1985 by Ratkovich + Levin. National Register + LA HCM.
- **Clue angle:** "What two street names combine to form 'Wiltern'?" / "What color are the tiles?" / "What movie studio ran the theater?"

### 3. The Talmadge
- **Address:** 3278 Wilshire Blvd (~0.15 mi north)
- **Year:** 1922-1924
- Named for silent film star Norma Talmadge. Husband Joseph Schenck (co-founder United Artists) bought building. Couple lived on 10th floor.
- Site previously held Craftsman mansion by Greene & Greene (1909) for auto dealer Earle C. Anthony. Mansion relocated to Beverly Hills by actor Norman Kerry (1923).
- Built by financier A.C. Blumenthal. 10 stories, 42 units. "Finest apartment building on the West Coast" at opening.
- **Clue angle:** "Who was Norma Talmadge?" / "What famous architects designed the house that stood here before?"

### 4. The Gaylord Apartments
- **Address:** 3355 Wilshire Blvd (~0.1 mi north)
- **Year:** Opened 1924
- Named for Henry Gaylord Wilshire — who gave Wilshire Boulevard its name. Renaissance Revival.
- Silent film stars lived here. Richard Nixon kept apartment during CA governor campaign.
- **Clue angle:** "This building shares a name with the man who named Wilshire Boulevard. What was his first name?" / "What U.S. president kept an apartment here?"

### 5. Ambassador Hotel Site / RFK Community Schools
- **Address:** 3400 Wilshire Blvd (~0.1 mi north)
- **Year:** Hotel opened Jan 1, 1921. Demolished 2005-2006.
- 1,200-room complex: 37 shops, golf course, bowling alley, 1,000-seat Cocoanut Grove nightclub (opened April 21, 1921). Cocoanut Grove hosted 6 Academy Awards ceremonies (1930-1943), including 1939 ceremony hosted by Bob Hope.
- June 5, 1968, 12:02 AM: RFK gave CA primary victory speech in Embassy Ballroom, shot by Sirhan Sirhan in kitchen pantry. Died at Good Samaritan Hospital ~25 hours later.
- LAUSD purchased 2001. Demolished 2005-06. Six schools opened 2009-10. RFK Inspiration Park on site.
- **Clue angle:** "What happened at 3400 Wilshire on June 5, 1968?" / "How many Oscar ceremonies were held in the nightclub?"

### 6. Wilshire Boulevard Temple
- **Address:** 3663 Wilshire Blvd (~0.2 mi NW)
- **Year:** Current building 1929; congregation founded 1862
- Oldest Jewish congregation in LA. Moorish/Byzantine Revival. Large dome. National Register 1981; LA HCM 1973.
- Interior murals by Hugo Ballin: 320 feet, depicting 3,000 years of Jewish history. Donated by Warner Bros founders Jack, Harry, Abraham Warner (in memory of brothers Milton and Samuel).
- Rabbi Edgar F. Magnin, "Rabbi to the Stars," served 69 years (1915-1984).
- **Clue angle:** "What movie studio founders paid for the 320-foot murals inside?" / "How long did the 'Rabbi to the Stars' serve?"

### 7. Western Avenue
- **Address:** Western Ave at Wilshire/6th St (~0.15 mi west)
- **Year:** Surveyed 1853 by Henry Washington
- Named because it was the westernmost edge of LA city expansion. 28-29 miles long — one of longest N-S streets in LA County (San Pedro to north LA).
- Name stuck even after city limit jumped past it in 1896 annexation.
- **Clue angle:** "Why is it called 'Western' if it's nowhere near the western edge?" / "How long is it?"

### 8. Hotel Normandie
- **Address:** 605 S Normandie Ave (~0.15 mi east)
- **Year:** 1926
- Renaissance Revival by Walker & Eisen. Originally residence hotel for single men.
- Malcolm Lowry wrote significant portions of "Under the Volcano" (1947) while living here — widely considered one of the greatest 20th-century novels.
- Purchased 2010 for $4.4M, restored for $5M into boutique hotel. LA HCM #1013 (2012). Cassell's Hamburgers (operating since 1948) moved to ground floor.
- **Clue angle:** "What famous novel was partly written in this hotel?" / "What year was the hamburger restaurant on the ground floor founded?"

### 9. Korean American National Museum
- **Address:** 3727 W 6th St (~0.15 mi east)
- **Year:** Founded 1991
- First and only institution for Korean American cultural heritage. Heart of largest ethnic Korean community outside Asia.
- Granted land at Vermont & 6th for permanent building.
- Koreatown officially designated 1980s; Korean settlement began 1960s.
- **Clue angle:** "What distinction does this neighborhood hold for the Korean diaspora?"

### 10. Da Wool Jung (Koreatown Pavilion Garden)
- **Address:** NE corner Olympic & Irolo (~0.3 mi south)
- **Year:** Opened January 2006
- 5,000 sq ft traditional Korean gazebo + garden. Built by South Korean craftsmen, $695K. Pine wood, traditional green/rust/red dyes, upswept tiled roof.
- Gateway monuments on Olympic topped with Bong Huang (mythical bird = prosperity/happiness in East Asian culture).
- **Clue angle:** "What mythical bird sits atop the Koreatown gateway arches?" / "What is the Korean name of the pavilion?"

### 11. A.W. Ross / Miracle Mile (contextual)
- **Address:** Wilshire between La Brea and Fairfax (~1.5 mi west — outside range)
- **Year:** 1921 (purchase), 1928 (name coined)
- A.W. Ross paid $54K for 18 acres. Detractors: "Ross's bean patch." Originally "Wilshire Boulevard Center."
- 1928: friend said "one would think this is really a miracle mile." Name stuck.
- Ross bet on automobile over streetcar — no Red Car on Wilshire. He was right.
- **Clue angle:** "Wilshire's most famous stretch was once called 'Ross's bean patch.' What developer bet $54K that cars would shape the city?"
